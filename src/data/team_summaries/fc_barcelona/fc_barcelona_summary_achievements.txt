### FC Barcelona: Historical Achievements Summary

#### 1. Major Trophies
FC Barcelona is one of the most successful football clubs in history, boasting an impressive array of trophies across domestic and international competitions:

- **La Liga Titles**: 28 (First won in 1929, most recently in 2024)
- **Copa del Rey Titles**: 32 (First won in 1910, most recently in 2025)
- **Supercopa de España Titles**: 15
- **UEFA Champions League Titles**: 5 (1991–92, 2005–06, 2008–09, 2010–11, 2014–15)
- **UEFA Cup Winners' Cup Titles**: 4 (1978–79, 1981–82, 1988–89, 1996–97)
- **UEFA Super Cup Titles**: 5
- **FIFA Club World Cup Titles**: 3 (2009, 2011, 2015)

#### 2. Records & Milestones
FC Barcelona has set numerous records and milestones throughout its history:

- **Lionel Messi**: The club's all-time top scorer with 672 goals and the highest appearance maker with 778 matches.
- **Most Goals in a Calendar Year**: Barcelona scored 180 goals in 2015, setting a record for most goals scored in a calendar year.
- **First European Club to Win a Treble Twice**: Achieved in 2009 and 2015.
- **Longest Unbeaten Run in La Liga**: 43 matches during the 2017–18 season.
- **First Team to Win Six Trophies in a Calendar Year**: Accomplished in 2009, known as the "Sextuple."

#### 3. Historical Significance
FC Barcelona is not just a football club; it has become a symbol of Catalan identity and culture. Founded in 1899, the club has evolved through various political and social landscapes, particularly during the Franco regime, where it represented resistance against oppression. The motto "Més que un club" (More than a club) encapsulates its role in Catalan nationalism and social issues.

Throughout its history, Barcelona has been a pioneer in youth development, with its famed La Masia academy producing world-class talents such as Xavi, Andrés Iniesta, and Lionel Messi. The club's commitment to playing attractive football, particularly during the "tiki-taka" era under Pep Guardiola (2008–2012), has influenced footballing philosophies worldwide.

FC Barcelona's rivalry with Real Madrid, known as "El Clásico," is one of the most famous in sports, reflecting deeper cultural and political tensions between Catalonia and Castile. The club's achievements and its cultural significance have made it a global brand, with a massive fan base and substantial financial success, despite recent challenges.

In summary, FC Barcelona's historical achievements are marked by a rich legacy of trophies, records, and a profound cultural significance that extends beyond the football pitch.

### Atlético Madrid: Historical Achievements Summary

#### Major Trophies
- **La Liga Titles**: 11 (1939–40, 1940–41, 1949–50, 1950–51, 1965–66, 1969–70, 1972–73, 1976–77, 1995–96, 2013–14, 2020–21)
- **Copa del Rey Titles**: 10 (1959–60, 1960–61, 1964–65, 1971–72, 1975–76, 1984–85, 1990–91, 1991–92, 1995–96, 2012–13)
- **Supercopa de España**: 2 (1985, 2014)
- **UEFA Europa League Titles**: 3 (2009–10, 2011–12, 2017–18)
- **UEFA Cup Winners' Cup**: 1 (1961–62)
- **UEFA Super Cup Titles**: 3 (2010, 2012, 2018)
- **Intercontinental Cup**: 1 (1974)

#### Records & Milestones
- Atlético Madrid was founded on April 26, 1903, initially as Athletic Club Sucursal de Madrid.
- The club became independent from Athletic Bilbao in 1921 and adopted its iconic red and white striped kit.
- Atlético has played in La Liga since its inception in 1929, with a total of 88 seasons in the top tier.
- The club reached the European Cup final in 1974 and has since made multiple appearances in UEFA Champions League finals, including in 2014 and 2016.
- Notable players include Luis Aragonés, Fernando Torres, and Antoine Griezmann, with Griezmann being the club's all-time top scorer with 197 goals.

#### Historical Significance
- Atlético Madrid has a rich history marked by periods of both triumph and struggle, reflecting the socio-political landscape of Spain.
- The club's identity is characterized by its working-class roots and a sense of rebellion, often contrasting with the more establishment image of its rivals, Real Madrid.
- The club has been a significant force in Spanish football, consistently challenging for titles and contributing to the competitive landscape of La Liga.
- Under the management of Diego Simeone since 2011, Atlético has experienced a resurgence, winning multiple trophies and establishing itself as a formidable competitor in both domestic and European football.

This structured overview highlights Atlético Madrid's historical achievements, showcasing its status as one of Spain's most storied football clubs.

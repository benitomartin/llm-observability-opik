# Real Madrid CF: Comprehensive Summary

## 1. Overview & History
Real Madrid Club de Fútbol, commonly known as Real Madrid, was founded on March 6, 1902, as Madrid Football Club. The club was granted the title "Real" (Royal) by King Alfonso XIII in 1920. It has a rich history marked by significant achievements and a strong cultural identity, often seen as a representation of Spanish football. The club's early years saw it become a founding member of the Royal Spanish Football Federation in 1909 and win its first Spanish Cup in 1905.

Under the presidency of Santiago Bernabéu from 1943 to 1978, Real Madrid experienced unprecedented success, winning multiple European Cups and establishing itself as a dominant force in both Spanish and European football. The club's "Galácticos" era began in the early 2000s under Florentino Pérez, characterized by high-profile signings like Luís Figo, Zinedine Zidane, and Cristiano Ronaldo.

## 2. Stadium & Facilities
Real Madrid's home ground is the Santiago Bernabéu Stadium, inaugurated on December 14, 1947. Initially named Nuevo Chamartín, it was renamed in honor of Santiago Bernabéu in 1955. The stadium has undergone several renovations, with a current capacity of approximately 81,044 spectators. It has hosted numerous significant events, including the 1982 FIFA World Cup final and several European Cup finals. The club's training facilities, known as Ciudad Real Madrid, include the Alfredo Di Stéfano Stadium, which has a capacity of 5,000 and serves as the home ground for Real Madrid Castilla.

## 3. Major Achievements
Real Madrid is one of the most successful football clubs in history, with a record number of titles, including:
- **La Liga**: 36 titles
- **Copa del Rey**: 20 titles
- **UEFA Champions League**: 15 titles (most in history)
- **FIFA Club World Cup**: 5 titles
- **UEFA Super Cup**: 6 titles

The club has also won numerous other domestic and international honors, solidifying its status as a global football powerhouse.

## 4. Notable Players & Management
Real Madrid has been home to many legendary players, including:
- **Cristiano Ronaldo**: All-time top scorer with 450 goals.
- **Alfredo Di Stéfano**: Integral to the club's success in the 1950s.
- **Raúl González**: Club legend with 741 appearances.
- **Karim Benzema**: Key player during the club's recent successes.

The management has seen notable figures such as:
- **Santiago Bernabéu**: Longest-serving president, instrumental in the club's growth.
- **Florentino Pérez**: Current president, known for the Galácticos policy.
- **Carlo Ancelotti**: Current manager, who has won multiple titles with the club.

## 5. Recent Performance
In recent years, Real Madrid has continued to perform at a high level, winning La Liga and the UEFA Champions League multiple times. The club secured its 14th Champions League title in 2022 and its 15th in 2024. The 2023-24 season saw Real Madrid win La Liga with 95 points and the Spanish Super Cup, showcasing a strong resurgence after the departure of key players like Cristiano Ronaldo.

## 6. Culture & Rivalries
Real Madrid's culture is deeply intertwined with its identity as a symbol of Spanish football. The club's anthem, "Hala Madrid," is a rallying cry for its supporters.

### Major Rivalries:
- **El Clásico**: The fierce rivalry with FC Barcelona, representing the historical and cultural tensions between Castile and Catalonia.
- **El Derbi madrileño**: The rivalry with Atlético Madrid, characterized by local pride and competition.
- **European Rivalries**: Notable rivalries with clubs like Bayern Munich and Juventus, often contested in the UEFA Champions League.

Real Madrid's fanbase is extensive, with a significant global following, and the club is known for its passionate supporters, including the Ultras Sur group, although they have faced controversies regarding their behavior.

### Recent Achievements (2020-2025)

Real Madrid has continued to solidify its status as one of the most successful clubs in football history through a series of remarkable achievements in recent years. In the 2021-22 season, under the management of Carlo Ancelotti, the club secured its 14th UEFA Champions League title, defeating Liverpool 1-0 in the final, thanks to a goal from Vinícius Júnior. This victory marked Real Madrid's fifth Champions League title in nine years, further enhancing their dominance in European football.

The 2022-23 season saw Real Madrid lift the Copa del Rey, adding to their extensive trophy cabinet. They also won the UEFA Super Cup and the FIFA Club World Cup during this period, although they faced setbacks in La Liga and the Spanish Super Cup, finishing behind rivals Barcelona.

In the 2023-24 season, Real Madrid achieved significant success by winning both the Spanish Super Cup and La Liga, amassing 95 points, which marked one of their best campaigns in recent history. They capped off the season by winning their 15th UEFA Champions League title, defeating Borussia Dortmund 2-0 in the final, with Dani Carvajal and Vinícius Júnior scoring the goals. This victory was notable as Real Madrid completed the tournament without losing a single match.

### Current Performance (2023-2025)

As of the 2023-24 season, Real Madrid has demonstrated exceptional performance in both domestic and international competitions. They have consistently ranked among the top teams in La Liga, showcasing a strong squad led by key players such as Jude Bellingham, Vinícius Júnior, and Thibaut Courtois. The team has maintained a high level of competitiveness, reaching the latter stages of the UEFA Champions League and consistently performing well in La Liga fixtures.

Real Madrid's tactical approach under head coach Xabi Alonso has emphasized a blend of youth and experience, allowing the team to adapt effectively to various match situations. The club's focus on integrating new signings, such as Bellingham and Aurélien Tchouaméni, has paid dividends, contributing to their success on the pitch.

### Latest Developments (2025)

In 2025, Real Madrid announced the highly anticipated signing of striker Kylian Mbappé on a free transfer from Paris Saint-Germain, concluding one of the most significant transfer sagas in modern football. This move is expected to bolster the team's attacking options and further enhance their chances of success in upcoming seasons.

Additionally, the club has continued to invest in its infrastructure, with ongoing renovations at the Santiago Bernabéu Stadium aimed at improving facilities for players and fans alike. The club's financial health remains robust, with significant revenue generated from merchandise sales, sponsorships, and matchday income, solidifying Real Madrid's position as one of the wealthiest and most valuable football clubs globally.

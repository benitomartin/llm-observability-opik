### Major Trophies

Paris Saint-Germain (PSG) has established itself as the most successful club in French football history, boasting an impressive collection of domestic and international trophies. As of the 2024–25 season, PSG's major honors include:

- **Ligue 1 Titles**: 13 (1985–86, 1993–94, 2012–13, 2013–14, 2014–15, 2015–16, 2017–18, 2018–19, 2019–20, 2021–22, 2022–23, 2023–24, 2024–25)
- **Coupe de France Titles**: 16 (1981–82, 1982–83, 1992–93, 1994–95, 1997–98, 2003–04, 2005–06, 2009–10, 2014–15, 2015–16, 2016–17, 2017–18, 2019–20, 2020–21, 2023–24, 2024–25)
- **Coupe de la Ligue Titles**: 9 (1994–95, 1997–98, 2007–08, 2013–14, 2014–15, 2015–16, 2016–17, 2017–18, 2019–20)
- **Trophée des Champions Titles**: 13 (1995, 1998, 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016, 2017, 2018, 2019, 2020, 2022, 2023, 2024)
- **Ligue 2 Title**: 1 (1970–71)
- **UEFA Champions League Title**: 1 (2024–25)
- **UEFA Cup Winners' Cup Title**: 1 (1995–96)
- **UEFA Intertoto Cup Title**: 1 (2001)

### Records & Milestones

PSG holds numerous records in French football, including:

- Most official titles won by a French club, totaling 50.
- Record holder for all national competitions, including the most Ligue 1 championships and Coupe de France titles.
- Only French club to win the UEFA Cup Winners' Cup, achieving this in 1996.
- Achieved the domestic quadruple (winning all four national titles in a single season) on four occasions.
- First team to win the Ligue 1 title while leading from the first to the last round (2022–23).
- Notable for having the longest consecutive presence in Ligue 1 since the 1974–75 season.

### Historical Significance

Founded in 1970 through a merger of Paris FC and Stade Saint-Germain, PSG quickly rose to prominence in French football. The club's historical significance is marked by its evolution from a fan-owned entity to a powerhouse under various ownerships, most notably the acquisition by Qatar Sports Investments (QSI) in 2011. This takeover transformed PSG into one of the wealthiest clubs globally, enabling record spending on player acquisitions and leading to domestic dominance.

PSG's rivalry with Olympique de Marseille, known as "Le Classique," is one of the most intense in football, reflecting the broader cultural and regional tensions between Paris and Marseille. The club's commitment to developing a strong footballing identity and its recent success in the UEFA Champions League further solidify its status as a major player on the European stage. The club's historical journey, marked by ups and downs, showcases its resilience and ambition, making it a significant chapter in the narrative of modern football.

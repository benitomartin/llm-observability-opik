# Paris Saint-Germain FC: Comprehensive Summary

## 1. Overview & History
Paris Saint-Germain Football Club (PSG) was founded on June 17, 1970, through the merger of Paris FC and Stade Saint-Germain. The club quickly rose through the ranks, achieving promotion to Division 1 in its inaugural season. Over the years, PSG has experienced various phases, including a split from Paris FC in 1972, a golden era following the Canal+ takeover in 1991, a decline in the late 1990s, and a resurgence under Qatari ownership starting in 2011. The club's identity is closely tied to the city of Paris and the royal town of Saint-Germain-en-Laye, represented by its colors: red, blue, and white.

## 2. Stadium & Facilities
PSG's home ground is the Parc des Princes, which has been their stadium since 1974. The stadium has a capacity of around 48,000 and is known for its vibrant atmosphere. In 2023, PSG opened Campus PSG in Poissy, a state-of-the-art training facility that replaced the historic Camp des Loges. This new complex accommodates the club's male and female teams, as well as various sports academies.

## 3. Major Achievements
Paris Saint-Germain is the most successful club in French football history, with a total of 50 official titles. Their honors include:
- **Ligue 1 Championships**: 13 titles (most recently in 2024-25)
- **Coupe de France**: 16 titles
- **Coupe de la Ligue**: 9 titles
- **Trophée des Champions**: 13 titles
- **UEFA Cup Winners' Cup**: 1 title (1995-96)
- **UEFA Intertoto Cup**: 1 title (2001)

In the 2024-25 season, PSG won their first UEFA Champions League title, completing a historic treble.

## 4. Notable Players & Management
Throughout its history, PSG has been home to numerous football legends, including:
- **Zlatan Ibrahimović**: A key figure during the QSI era, known for his goal-scoring prowess.
- **Ronaldinho**: One of the club's early stars who helped elevate its profile.
- **Kylian Mbappé**: The club's all-time leading goalscorer and a pivotal player in recent successes.
- **Lionel Messi**: Joined PSG in 2021, adding to the club's star power.

The current management includes President **Nasser Al-Khelaifi** and head coach **Luis Enrique**, who took charge in July 2023.

## 5. Recent Performance
Under QSI ownership, PSG has dominated French football, winning multiple domestic titles and consistently reaching the knockout stages of the UEFA Champions League. However, the club faced criticism for not securing the Champions League trophy until 2025. The 2024-25 season marked a turning point, with PSG adopting a team-first approach and winning the Champions League in a decisive 5-0 victory over Inter Milan.

## 6. Culture & Rivalries
PSG has a passionate fanbase, with an estimated 35 million supporters globally. The club's most intense rivalry is with **Olympique de Marseille**, known as **Le Classique**, which is considered the biggest fixture in French football. The rivalry has historical roots and has intensified over the years, particularly during the 1990s and 2000s. PSG's supporters are known for their vibrant atmosphere at the Parc des Princes, with various ultra groups contributing to the club's culture and identity.

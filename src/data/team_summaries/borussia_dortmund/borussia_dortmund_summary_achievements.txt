## Borussia Dortmund: Historical Achievements

### Major Trophies
Borussia Dortmund, one of Germany's most storied football clubs, has a rich history of success both domestically and internationally. The club's major achievements include:

- **Bundesliga Titles**: 8 (1956, 1957, 1963, 1994–95, 1995–96, 2001–02, 2010–11, 2011–12)
- **DFB-Pokal (German Cup)**: 5 (1964–65, 1988–89, 2011–12, 2016–17, 2020–21)
- **DFL-Supercup**: 6 (1989, 1995, 1996, 2013, 2014, 2019)
- **UEFA Champions League**: 1 (1996–97)
- **UEFA Cup Winners' Cup**: 1 (1965–66)
- **Intercontinental Cup**: 1 (1997)

### Records & Milestones
Borussia Dortmund has set numerous records throughout its history, showcasing its competitive edge and resilience:

- **First Bundesliga Goal**: Friedhelm Konietzka scored the first-ever goal in Bundesliga history in 1963.
- **Most Goals in a UEFA Champions League Match**: Dortmund holds the record for the most goals scored in a single Champions League match, netting 12 goals in an 8-4 victory over Legia Warsaw in the 2016-17 season.
- **Youngest Player in Bundesliga History**: Youssoufa Moukoko made his debut at 16 years and 1 day old, becoming the youngest player ever to play in the Bundesliga.
- **Most Appearances**: Michael Zorc is the club's all-time appearance leader with 572 matches played.
- **Record Attendance**: Dortmund has the highest average attendance of any football club worldwide, with its home matches at Signal Iduna Park regularly drawing large crowds.

### Historical Significance
Founded on December 19, 1909, Borussia Dortmund has evolved from modest beginnings into a powerhouse of German football. The club's historical significance is marked by its:

- **Cultural Impact**: Dortmund has a passionate fan base and is known for its vibrant atmosphere, particularly at Signal Iduna Park, which is renowned for its "Yellow Wall" of supporters.
- **Rivalries**: The club has intense rivalries, most notably with Schalke 04, known as the Revierderby, which is one of the fiercest rivalries in German football.
- **International Recognition**: Dortmund's success in European competitions, especially winning the UEFA Champions League in 1997, has established it as a respected name on the international stage.
- **Financial Resilience**: Despite facing financial difficulties in the early 2000s, including near bankruptcy, the club successfully restructured and returned to prominence, demonstrating its resilience and commitment to its fans.

Overall, Borussia Dortmund's history is characterized by a legacy of triumph, a commitment to community, and a passionate fan culture that continues to thrive today.

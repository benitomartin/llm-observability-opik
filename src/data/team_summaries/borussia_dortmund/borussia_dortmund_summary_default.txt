# Borussia Dortmund: Comprehensive Summary

## 1. Overview & History
Borussia Dortmund, officially known as Ballspiel-Verein Borussia 09 e.V. Dortmund, was founded on December 19, 1909, by a group of young men discontent with the local church-sponsored football club. The name "Borussia" is derived from the Latin term for Prussia and was inspired by a local brewery. The club initially wore blue and white striped shirts before adopting their iconic black and yellow colors in 1913.

The club faced financial difficulties in the late 1920s and was nearly bankrupt, but survived due to the support of local benefactors. After World War II, Borussia Dortmund emerged as a competitive force in German football, achieving their first national title in 1956. They became a founding member of the Bundesliga in 1963 and have since established themselves as one of Germany's most successful clubs.

## 2. Stadium & Facilities
Borussia Dortmund's home ground is the Signal Iduna Park, formerly known as Westfalenstadion, which is the largest stadium in Germany and the seventh-largest in Europe, with a capacity of 81,359 for league matches. The stadium has undergone several renovations, including expansions for the 2006 FIFA World Cup. The club also boasts a state-of-the-art training facility located in Brackel, which includes multiple pitches, rehabilitation areas, and advanced training technology.

## 3. Major Achievements
Borussia Dortmund has a rich history of success, including:
- **Bundesliga Titles**: 8 (1956, 1957, 1963, 1995, 1996, 2002, 2011, 2012)
- **DFB-Pokal Titles**: 5 (1965, 1989, 2012, 2017, 2021)
- **DFL-Supercup Titles**: 6 (1989, 1995, 1996, 2013, 2014, 2019)
- **UEFA Champions League**: 1 (1997)
- **UEFA Cup Winners' Cup**: 1 (1966)
- **Intercontinental Cup**: 1 (1997)

The club is also noted for its strong youth development and has produced several notable players who have gone on to achieve international acclaim.

## 5. Recent Performance
In recent years, Borussia Dortmund has remained competitive in both domestic and European competitions. They finished second in the Bundesliga in the 2022-23 season, narrowly missing out on the title to Bayern Munich. The 2023-24 season saw them finish fifth in the Bundesliga but reach the UEFA Champions League final for the first time in over a decade, where they lost to Real Madrid.

## 6. Culture & Rivalries
Borussia Dortmund has a passionate fan base and is known for its vibrant atmosphere, particularly in the "Yellow Wall," the famous standing terrace at Signal Iduna Park. The club has a fierce rivalry with Schalke 04, known as the Revierderby, which is one of the most intense rivalries in German football. Additionally, their matches against Bayern Munich, dubbed "Der Klassiker," are highly anticipated fixtures that often have significant implications for the Bundesliga title race.

In summary, Borussia Dortmund is a storied club with a rich history, a strong competitive spirit, and a dedicated fan base, making it a cornerstone of German football.

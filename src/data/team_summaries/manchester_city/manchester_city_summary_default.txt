# Manchester City F.C. Summary

## 1. Overview & History
Manchester City Football Club, founded in 1880 as St. Marks (Gorton), has a rich history marked by periods of success and decline. The club gained its first major trophy in 1904 by winning the FA Cup, becoming the first Manchester club to do so. Over the decades, City experienced a golden era in the late 1960s and early 1970s, winning multiple domestic and European titles. However, the club faced significant challenges in the 1980s and 1990s, including relegation to lower divisions. A resurgence began in the early 2000s, culminating in a transformative takeover by the Abu Dhabi United Group in 2008, which led to substantial investment in players and infrastructure.

## 2. Stadium & Facilities
Manchester City plays its home matches at the City of Manchester Stadium, commonly known as the Etihad Stadium, which has been the club's home since 2003. The stadium underwent significant renovations to accommodate football, including lowering the pitch and adding seating. It currently has a capacity of 55,097, with plans for expansion to 61,000. The club also operates the Etihad Campus, which includes training facilities and the City Football Academy.

## 3. Major Achievements
Manchester City is one of the most successful clubs in English football, boasting a total of 36 major domestic, European, and worldwide honors. Key achievements include:
- **Premier League Titles**: 10 (most recently in 2023-24)
- **FA Cups**: 7 (most recently in 2022-23)
- **League Cups**: 8 (most recently in 2020-21)
- **UEFA Champions League**: 1 (2022-23)
- **European Cup Winners' Cup**: 1 (1969-70)
- **FIFA Club World Cup**: 1 (2023)

City also achieved a historic domestic treble in the 2022-23 season, winning the Premier League, FA Cup, and UEFA Champions League.

## 5. Recent Performance
In recent years, Manchester City has dominated English football, winning six out of seven Premier League titles from 2017-18 to 2023-24. The 2022-23 season was particularly remarkable, as City secured a treble, including their first UEFA Champions League title. The club continues to perform strongly in domestic and European competitions.

## 6. Culture & Rivalries
Manchester City has a passionate fanbase, with a significant following both locally and globally. The club's anthem, "Blue Moon," is sung fervently by supporters. The main rivalry is with local neighbors Manchester United, known as the Manchester derby, which has intensified in recent years due to both clubs' competitive successes. Additionally, City has developed a notable rivalry with Liverpool, particularly in the context of recent title races and high-stakes matches. Other rivalries include those with Tottenham Hotspur and Chelsea, reflecting the competitive nature of modern English football.

Overall, Manchester City F.C. has established itself as a powerhouse in both domestic and European football, characterized by a rich history, modern facilities, and a vibrant culture among its supporters.

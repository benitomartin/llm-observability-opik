### Manchester City F.C. Historical Achievements

#### 1. Major Trophies
Manchester City Football Club has a rich history of success, with a total of **36 major domestic, European, and worldwide honors**, making them one of the most decorated clubs in England. Their major trophies include:

- **First Division / Premier League Titles:** 10 titles (1936–37, 1967–68, 2011–12, 2013–14, 2017–18, 2018–19, 2020–21, 2021–22, 2022–23, 2023–24)
- **FA Cup Titles:** 7 titles (1903–04, 1933–34, 1955–56, 1968–69, 2010–11, 2018–19, 2022–23)
- **Football League Cup / EFL Cup Titles:** 8 titles (1969–70, 1975–76, 2013–14, 2015–16, 2017–18, 2018–19, 2019–20, 2020–21)
- **FA Charity Shield / FA Community Shield Titles:** 7 titles (1937, 1968, 1972, 2012, 2018, 2019, 2024)
- **UEFA Champions League Titles:** 1 title (2022–23)
- **European Cup Winners' Cup Titles:** 1 title (1969–70)
- **UEFA Super Cup Titles:** 1 title (2023)
- **FIFA Club World Cup Titles:** 1 title (2023)

Additionally, City achieved a historic **domestic treble** in the 2018–19 season by winning the Premier League, FA Cup, and League Cup, and replicated this feat in the 2022–23 season, adding the UEFA Champions League to their accolades.

#### 2. Records & Milestones
Manchester City holds several notable records and milestones in English football:

- **Most Goals in a Single Season:** Erling Haaland scored 52 goals in the 2022–23 season.
- **Highest Points Total in Premier League History:** City recorded 100 points in the 2017–18 season.
- **Largest Home Attendance:** The club's highest recorded attendance was 84,569 during an FA Cup tie against Stoke City in 1934.
- **First Team to Win All Major English Trophies in a Single Season:** Achieved in the 2018–19 season.
- **First English Men's Club to Win Four Consecutive League Titles:** Accomplished in the 2023–24 season.

#### 3. Historical Significance
Founded in 1880 as St. Mark's (Gorton), Manchester City has evolved into a powerhouse in English football. The club's historical significance is marked by its resilience through various phases, including early successes with the FA Cup in 1904, a golden era in the late 1960s and early 1970s, and a resurgence following the 2008 takeover by the Abu Dhabi United Group.

The club's identity is deeply intertwined with the Manchester community, and its rivalry with Manchester United is one of the fiercest in football. Over the years, City has transformed from a club facing relegation and financial difficulties to a dominant force in both domestic and European competitions, reflecting the changing landscape of football in England and the impact of investment in sports. The arrival of Pep Guardiola in 2016 marked the beginning of a new golden era, characterized by attractive football and consistent trophy success, solidifying Manchester City's place in football history.

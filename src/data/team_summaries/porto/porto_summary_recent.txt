### Recent Achievements (2020-2025)

FC Porto has enjoyed a successful period from 2020 to 2023, marked by significant domestic triumphs. In the 2019-20 season, they secured their 29th Primeira Liga title and won the Taça de Portugal, marking their first cup victory in eleven years. The following season, 2020-21, Porto reached the quarter-finals of the UEFA Champions League, defeating Juventus on away goals in the round of 16, although they ended the season with only the Supertaça trophy.

The 2021-22 season was particularly remarkable as Porto reclaimed the Primeira Liga title with a record 91 points and established a new league record of 58 consecutive matches unbeaten. They also won the Taça de Portugal, achieving a domestic double. In January 2023, Porto won their first-ever Taça da Liga title by defeating Sporting CP, completing a clean sweep of national trophies.

### Current Performance (2023-2025)

As of the 2023-2024 season, FC Porto has continued to perform competitively, finishing third in the Primeira Liga. They have maintained a strong squad with players like Evanilson as their top scorer. Porto's performance in European competitions has seen them reach the round of 16 in the UEFA Champions League, although they have not advanced beyond this stage in recent seasons. The club remains a formidable force in Portuguese football, consistently competing for top honors domestically.

### Latest Developments (2025)

In April 2024, André Villas-Boas was elected as the new president of FC Porto, succeeding Jorge Nuno Pinto da Costa. His election, which garnered 80% of the vote, signals a new era for the club. Villas-Boas has already initiated changes, including the appointment of Vitor Bruno as the new head coach. Additionally, he has introduced a women's football team to represent FC Porto and is focused on integrating younger players from the B team and U-19 squad into the first team. These developments aim to strengthen the club's future and enhance its connection with the fanbase.
